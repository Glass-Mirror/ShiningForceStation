Max Swordsman (SDMN) → Hero (HERO)

Max is the main character of the game. He is the leader of the shining force and whenever you are out of battle you play as him.
You can name him whatever you want, but leave it blank, and he'll be called Max.
Max is all around a pretty good fighter, not majorly lagging or excelling in any stat category. He'll only ever learn the spell Egress, regardless of his random MP stat increases.
Since the battle is lost if Max dies, some might be worried about using him. But for this very reason, he can fall behind, putting him in more danger of dying. As long as he is kept up with the force and isn't allowed to fall behind, he can be used as a fighter without too much worry.

Luke Warrior (WARR) → Gladiator (GLDT)

Luke is one of the original force. He joins before your first battle. He tends to grow pretty well, at ends up good for the front lines. His ATT and DEF grow enough that he never is really useless. He is consistent and a wonderful warrior.
Personally, I really like Luke, and use him in most playthroughs, I get him early, meaning I can easily keep his level up with everyone else. And his DEF and HP grow quickly enough that I don't worry about his death constatntly, not a glass cannnon.
Luke, like all the initial party members, it is possible to skip obtaining Luke, if you perform some manual RNG manipulation.

Ken Knight (KNT) → Paladin (PLDN)

Ken is one of the original force. He joins before your first battle. He's ok. Early on, he grows quick enough to not become obsolete. But, by the end of chapter 3, he seems to lose this edge. If you push through, he'll end up good though. His stats focus on HP, he'll get tons of it, though, this occurs while his DEF fails to grow half decently.
Personally, I don't really like Ken. He gets really high HP and his ATT gets good. But his DEF sucks forever, and I prefer other centaurs and knight-like characters.
Ken, like all the initial party members, it is possible to skip obtaining Ken, if you perform some manual RNG manipulation.

Tao is one of the original force. She joins before your first battle. She's a mage, and really excels early game. Her blaze spells guarantee she can deal at least 6 damage, regardless of the enemy, and up to 9 on some.
I really want to like Tao. She's so good early game. But if you want a mage, you've got better options. She's the only mage to learn blaze level 4, of all the 3 elemental magic spells, blaze is the worst. It deal extra to the undead enemies, but late game, these enemies become scarce (though the are in the final battle)
Tao, like all the initial party members, it is possible to skip obtaining Tao, if you perform some manual RNG manipulation.

Lowe Healer (HEAL) → Vicar (VICR)

Lowe is one of the original force. He joins before your first battle. As a healer, he's integral to your force early on. Luckily you'll quickly get better options for your healer, since he'll never be more than mediocre. He does learn a variety of useful spells, but his low defense makes him glass, requiring he be constantly protected.
Lowe isn't really that good. You have other options for a healer, all of the better. If you want someone who is decent in a variety of areas, then maybe consider him. But if you need a healer with any specific traits, look elsewhere.
Lowe, like all the initial party members, it is possible to skip obtaining Lowe, if you perform some manual RNG manipulation.

Gong Monk (MONK) → Master Monk (MMNK)

Gong is the first optional character in the game. If you are playing with no glitches or exploits, he's the first character you can miss.
You can get him on your team if you talk to him outside his hut (Gong's hut) just East of the Gate of the Anceients in Guardiana. If you excape from your first battle, you can have him in your first battle. Otherwise, get him after the first battle, and before the second.
I love Gong. He's able to heal, and can simultaneously heal people, and in his later levels, you shouldn't have too much of a MP issue, though early on it can be a bit low.
I always suggest Gong. Since you can keep him on the front lines, he's always nearby when someone needs healing. And you shouldn't have any issue keeping up in levels, since he can also gain from fighting and not just healing.

Gort Warrior (WARR) → Gladiator (GLDT)

Gort is another optional character. If you are a big explorer, he's hard to miss. But if you just push through the game, ignoring the non-battle segments, you'll probably go the whole game without ever having got him.
If you want to get him, you'll need to find him after the 2nd battle. He's sitting in the bar in Gaurdiana. The bar is on the East side of town, North-East from the shop.
He starts better the Luke, and isn't bad. I find him really useful, due to his tendency to take little damage from mechanical enemies.

Mae Knight (KNT) → Paladin (PLDN)

Mae is the daughter of your trainer Varios. You can meet her before the first battle, but she won't join until after the second. You can't miss her.
She is better than Ken. While Ken gets more HP, she compensates by getting tons of DEF. She becomes a tank if you are careful.
I suggest you use her as one of your centaurs. Sure, her movement sucks in forest and on mountains, but she can take so much damage. Her HP rises really well later in the game, though early on you'll want to keep an eye on her where there are lots of magic casters.

Khris is an average healer, but I find it way to easy to completely lsoe her. Leave her out of a battle or two, fail to keep her busy, and you'll spend the next few battles making sure she isn't too far behind.

Anri Mage (MAGE) → Wizard (WIZD)

Anri is the the King of Guardiana's daughter, and she is in the mage school of Mariana. While she first is hesitant to join you, as she must grieve her father's death, if you go find her after she runs off, she'll join the force.
Anri is the 2nd mage you encounter and is largely preferrable to Tao, as Anri learns Freeze. She'll learn all the way to leave 4, which deals massive damage to a single opponenet.
I largely prefer her to Tao. Tao learns a better variety of attack spells, and is, as a result, more usuable in my runs. She learns up to level 2 in blaze and bolt, and level 4 freeze. As a result, she has 2 levels of blaze available for early on undead enemies.

Arthur Knight (KNTE) → Paladin (PLDN)

Arthur is a unique character. He's found in the mage school, Mariana. He's found washing laundry on one of the upper of levels of the building. Though you can talk to him before Battle 2-2, he won't join you until after.
Arthur is unique because he is the only knight that can learn magic spells. Learning blaze, freeze, and bolt all to level 1, this allows him to deal some magic damage if needed. While this can be useful in early levels, and perhaps later, once he has bolt 1, he'll eventually outgrow the spells, doing more damage without than with, making them all around useless.
I never really use Arthur, he's just too much of a pain to build up. He starts ridiculously low, and takes forever to catch him up. Furthermore his stats start low and slow. If you do catch him up though, I hear he can be one of the best knights in the game.

Balbaroy Birdman (BDMN) → Sky Warrior (SKYW)

Balbaroy is the first birdman you'll meet in the game. You'll first find him in Shaddey Abbey, being used as bait for an ambush.
He's the first character other than max that can use most swords. Furthermore, he can fly. Due to his flying taking away his land bonuses, he can take lots of damage if you aren't careful, but leveled up properly, and he'll be an invaluable member of the team.
I've used Balbaroy. But he takes more damage than I like, making him harder to use than many other characters. It doesn't help that the first battle you can use him in, the enemies most accessible enemies can, and will, one shot him.

Jogurt Yogurt (YGRT)

Arguably the best charater in the game. He comes ready to promote and with as high stats as he'll ever get - the only way to increase them is using items like the bread of life or the power potion.
Jogurt is one of the hidden charaters, first encountered at Gong's Hut in Chapter 1. He can be found a second time in Bustoke at some point, though he won't join the force until Chapter 4, where he is hiding just north west of the church after Pao leaves the first time. For details on how to get Jorgurt, see the secrets page.
I find Jogurt as an indespensible part of every team. He acts as the games mascot, and every kill he makes causes the Jorgurt Ring to be dropped.

For details on how to get Jorgurt, see the secrets page. I find Jogurt as an indespensible part of every team. He acts as the games mascot, and every kill he makes causes the Jorgurt Ring to be dropped.

Kokichi Wing Knight (WKNT) → Sky Lord (SKYL)

A unique character for sure. Kokichi is a optional character, and fills the role of a flying knight. He can use spears and lances like all other knights, and flys, therefore making him ignore land effect.
Being the only flying knight, Kokichi fills a unique spot in the game. Most knights are centaurs, and as such recieve high land effects in mountains and forest. His ability to quickly move around even in high land effects, makes he a mobile knight.
I don't really use him. Not because he is bad, but because leveling him up is a bit difficult, though if you level him properly, he can be all around quite good.

Vankar Paladin (PLDN)

Vankar is another Centaur. Nothing really special. You can grab him after Pao leaves the first time. If you wander around in the fields you'll find him standing around.
If you bother to keep him up with the force, he'll end up mid-tier in almost all his stats, excelling in no category. But, if you need another centaur, he's there. Additionally, he comes just in time for most effortless grind for range characters in the game (at the end of the chapter).
I personally don't use him, I have other better lance users, and I really dislike centaurs because of their movement restrictions. But, I would use him over Ken every time.

Guntz Steam Knight (SKNT) → Steam Baron (SBRN)

A special character, Guntz is another unique class character. He is another knight, using lances and spears, but ignores lots of land effect penalties that the centaurs face. He's found in the 2nd Pao, in the sheep pen. Talk to hin, and he'll join the force.
Guntz is a beast when it comes to defense. He gets it in loads, often ending with the best defense of anyone on the force. While he lacks in little in the HP department, his defense easily compensates. He also gets great attack. But, with a movement of only 4, I suggest giving him the Mobility Ring
I always use Guntz. His massive defense and great attack means he can face late game enemies with easy. And you get him in time for the most effortless EXP grind for range characters (at the end of this chapter). I always suggest using him.

Domingo Magic Creature (MGCR)

Domingo is yet another distinctive character. Arguably another mascot for the game in fact. He is obtained by finding the Domingo Egg from the fake monster in Manarina (Chapter 2). The bringing the egg to a scientist in the shop of the 2nd Pao. He'll heat the egg, causing it to hatch. And you'll get Domingo, another must have character.
Domingo is another mage, focusing mainly on the freeze series. Though he'll learn a few other various spells. He joins the force at Lv. 1, but since he can't be promoted, this can be assumed as Lv. 1, post-promotion. Due to being a magic user, he can gain levels rather quickly, and his over all great defense later on makes him a great asset. Being the only mage to gain decent defense puts Domingo in a unique position, and his floating abiltiy gives him better movement than most other characters.
I always use Domingo, he's great. As soon as I get him, I'll drop Anri, because I find Domingo to be a better Freeze mage. Growing better in all his stats, and not needing constant protection due to low DF.

Lyle Assault Knight (ASKT) → Strike Knight (SKNT)

Lyle is found in Rudo. He's got the trait of being a centaur archer. Which is interesting. He's got the movement of a centaur, while also being able to equip archer weapons. Often times he makes a good replacement for Diane or Hans, since he is often better than them at the time of joining.

Alef Mage (MAGE) → Wizard (WIZD)

Alef is a female half-fox spellcaster. She left Prompt with Torasu to stop Darksol at the Tower of the Ancients. But they aren't able to do it own their own, and need your help. After finishing the battles on the Tower, she'll join your party.
A pretty good spellcaster, she's the bolt caster. Meaning she'll learn all the way up to Bolt 4, the most powerful spell in the game. She also learn a variety of other attack spells, though none as well as bolt. She ends with high MP, and low everything else.
I have tried to use her, and have succeeded for a couple battles, but I find her DF and HP are too low. I can't rely on her not being killed, so she's usually left off the team.
